classic sourdough

description

a classic sourdough recipe using just 4 ingredients.

ingredients

  • 900g bread flour
  • 660g lukewarm water
  • 18g salt
  • 100g sourdough starter

steps

  1. the night before, or roughly 8 hours before mixing, feed your starter a 1:1 mix of flour and water
  2. in a large bowl, mix the ripe sourdough starter with water, stirring to dissolve
  3. add the flour and salt, mixing until throughouly incorporated. ensure there is no dry flour
  4. let the dough rest covered for 30 minutes
  5. once rested, fold the dough in the bowl from the sides to the center. you can also pick the dough up and perform a coil-fold on the dough
  6. once the gluten has started to tense up and resist, let sit in the bowl for another 30 minutes
  7. repeat steps 5 and 6 two more times, resting for 30 minutes between folds
  8. after the final coil fold, let the dough rest in the bowl, covered, until bulk ferment is complete. a handy table constructed by bill wraith of 'thefreshloaf.com' can be used to estimate bulk rise time
  9. once the dough has fermented and gained size, turn the dough out onto a clean, floured work surface
  10. cut the dough into two equally sized pieces
  11. preshape by carefully folding the dough into a ball, starting from the edges and bringing them to the center. then, form the dough into a ball.
  12. let rest uncovered for 30 minutes to relax the gluten
  13. once dough is rested, flip it over to have the sticky side up and then stretch it into a square, careful as to not disturb the air pockets
  14. perform final shaping. i make two loaves, one batard and one boule. a boule is formed the same way as the preshape ball, but this time after forming it into a ball, roll the dough on the counter towards you with a cupped motion using both hands. this will tighten the dough
  15. a batard is formed by folding the dough in thirds towards the center like a letter, then rolling it into a swiss-roll shape
  16. place the shaped dough into floured bannetons. a banneton is a vessel for holding dough while it completes its proof that keeps the dough from losing its shape
  17. place the bannetons in the fridge to cold proof overnight, or roughly 12-18 hours
  18. once the dough has proofed, preheat an oven to 500f/260c with a dutch oven inside
  19. remove the dough from the fridge, and remove it from the bannetons and place it on a cut sheet of parchment paper that will fit the dutch oven.
  20. using a sharp razor blade or knife, score along the center of the dough at a 15 degree angle, making a slight crescent shape from one end to the other
  21. place the dough gently into the dutch oven, add an ice cube into the dutch oven underneath the parchment paper to introduce steam. cover with a lid and bake for 22 minutes
  22. after the timer expires, remove the lid and reduce temp to 450f/230c and bake for an additional 20 minutes to develop the crust
  23. remove the bread, and let rest on a bread rack for at least 1 hour before serving
  24. repeat steps 19 to 23 for the remaining loaf
